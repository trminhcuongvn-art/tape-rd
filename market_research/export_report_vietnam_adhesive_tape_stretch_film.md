# Vietnam Export Market Report — Adhesive Tape & Stretch/Shrink Film

**Date:** 2026-05-30  
**Prepared by:** Cáo — Technical Advisor  
**Scope:** Export opportunities for Vietnam-made adhesive tape and PE stretch/shrink film.

## Executive Summary

Vietnam exports two related but distinct packaging product groups:

1. **Plastic self-adhesive tape/film — HS 3919**  
   Includes BOPP/OPP carton sealing tape, jumbo rolls and other self-adhesive plastic flat shapes.

2. **PE stretch/shrink film — HS 3920.10 / 392010**  
   Includes polyethylene stretch wrap, pallet wrap and shrink film, provided the product is non-cellular and not reinforced/laminated/combined with other materials.

**Initial market signal:** HS 3919 already shows meaningful export demand from Vietnam. Public OEC search snippets indicate key destination markets include **South Korea, India, China, the United States and Brazil**. TrendEconomy snippets indicate Vietnam exports of HS 3919 reached about **USD 219 million in 2021**.

**Key caution:** Pakistan has opened an anti-dumping investigation into Vietnamese BOPP self-adhesive jumbo rolls under Pakistan HS **3919.9010 / 3919.9090**. Pakistan should be treated as a high-risk market until the case is clarified.

## 1. Product Scope & HS Classification

### 1.1 Adhesive Tape — HS 3919

**HS 3919 definition:** Self-adhesive plates, sheets, film, foil, tape, strip and other flat shapes, of plastics, whether or not in rolls.

Typical products:
- BOPP/OPP carton sealing tape.
- Transparent/brown/colored packing tape.
- Jumbo rolls for converting.
- Self-adhesive plastic film or sheets.

Required export description:
- Base material: BOPP, OPP, PET, PVC, PE.
- Adhesive type: acrylic, hot-melt, rubber-based.
- Form: jumbo roll or finished roll.
- Width, length, film thickness, adhesive thickness, core size.
- Printed/unprinted, color, packaging method.

### 1.2 PE Stretch/Shrink Film — HS 3920.10 / 392010

**HS 392010 definition:** Plates, sheets, film, foil and strip, of polymers of ethylene, non-cellular and not reinforced, laminated, supported or similarly combined with other materials.

Typical products:
- Hand-grade stretch film.
- Machine-grade stretch film.
- Pallet wrap.
- PE shrink film / heat-shrinkable film.

Required export description:
- Stretch film vs shrink film.
- Resin: LLDPE, LDPE, PE.
- Thickness in microns.
- Roll width, roll length, roll net/gross weight.
- Stretch ratio/pre-stretch if applicable.
- Printed/unprinted, color, multilayer status.

**Classification warning:** If the film is self-adhesive, it may move to HS 3919. If it is composite, foam, bag-form or pipe-form, other HS headings such as 3921, 3923 or 3917 may apply.

## 4. Trade Remedy Risk

### Pakistan anti-dumping case — BOPP tape

Pakistan’s National Tariff Commission has initiated an anti-dumping investigation against BOPP self-adhesive jumbo rolls from Vietnam.

Key details:
- Product: BOPP self-adhesive tape in jumbo roll form.
- Pakistan HS: 3919.9010 and 3919.9090.
- Allegation period: 2025.
- Injury review period: 2023–2025.

Recommendations:
- Do not prioritize Pakistan for BOPP jumbo rolls without legal review.
- If selling to Pakistan, preserve cost sheets, invoices, contracts, export prices and production records.
- Avoid aggressive below-cost pricing that could worsen anti-dumping exposure.

## 5. Export Documentation Checklist

Minimum document pack:
- Sales contract / purchase order.
- Commercial invoice.
- Packing list.
- Bill of lading / air waybill.
- Certificate of origin where FTA preference is used.
- Technical datasheet.
- Certificate of analysis if required.
- MSDS if adhesive/chemical details are requested.
- Shipping marks and pallet/carton labels.

FTA/C/O checks:
- ASEAN: Form D / ATIGA.
- China: Form E / ACFTA or RCEP.
- Korea: Form AK / VKFTA.
- Japan: VJFTA, AJCEP or RCEP.
- EU: EVFTA origin documentation.
- Australia/New Zealand: AANZFTA, CPTPP or RCEP as applicable.

- Product photos and container-loading photos.
- Sample policy.

### PE stretch/shrink film

Recommended products:
1. Hand-roll stretch film for SMEs/logistics.
2. Machine-grade stretch film for warehouses/factories.
3. Shrink film for food/FMCG if compliance is available.
4. OEM/private-label rolls for distributors.

Differentiation points:
- Real net film weight, not heavy-core selling.
- Stable micron thickness.
- High puncture resistance.
- Good cling and stretch ratio.
- Low breakage rate on machines.
